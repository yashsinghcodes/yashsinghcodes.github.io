So what is it?
It's a branch of chemistry that deals with the speed of the chemical reactions at least that's how I understand this.
There is the term rate of reaction which could be defined as the change in the molar concentration of a reactant or product per unit time or increase/decrease in molar concentration of reaction per unit time. It can be matematically be represented as:

-▲R/ ▲t = ▲P/▲t

For a reaction
R ---> P
We can simply represent as:
Rate of Reaction = Change in molar concentation/Time taken

The rate of reaction is divided into two categories

  • Average rate of reaction: Change in molar concentration of R or P(Reference from above example) in a given interval of time.

  • Instantaneous rate of reaction: Change in molar concentration of R or P(Reference from above example) at a given instant of time.
It is differentiated by delta(▲) and d

For example:
aA + bB -----> cC
For average :

-1/a ▲[A]/▲t = -1/b ▲[B]/▲t = 1/ c ▲[C]/▲t

For Instantaneous:

-1/a d[A]/dt = -1/b d[B]/dt = 1/ c d[C]/dt

Now we can talk about factor which affects the rate of reaction
  • Temperature
  • Catalyst
  • Physical state of the reactant
  • Sunlight
  • Concentration of reaction
  • Surface area of the reactant

Now its time to discuss Rate Law

According to Rate Law rate of a chemical reaction is directly proportional to the product of the molar concentration of reactant each raises to the power their actual used concentration term on which rate of reaction depends.

Mathematically it can be written as:
Rate of reaction ∝ [A]α> [B]ß

For the chemical reaction:

aA+ bB -----> Product


Proportionality sign can be removed by introducing a constant which we call Rate constant or Specific reaction rate.


Now the order of reaction can be defined by the sum of powers on which rate of reaction depends according to rate law expression.
